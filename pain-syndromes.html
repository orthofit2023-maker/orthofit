BUNION/HALLUX VALGUS


It's a bony bump at the base of your big toe, caused by uneven pressure on the 1st metatarsal during weight-bearing activities. This makes the big toe drift toward the second toe, swelling the joint and making it stick out.

Over time, the joint gets inflamed and painful and your foot widens making difficult/struggle to wear open or close footwear.

Bunions don't reverse once they're develop. The products, care and treatment are focused on prevention, not a fix:

  • Stopping it from getting worse
  • Managing pain
  • Prevention of calluses and corn

  • Swelling, redness and tenderness around the bump
  • Develop calluses and corn under the bumps and between overlapping toes
  • Less movement in the toe joint

  • Genetics (runs in the family)
  • Weak, reduced or collapsed medial and transverse arches
  • Wearing ill-fitting, tight, pointy, or high-heeled shoes
  • Arthritis or joint issues

  • Strengthen those medial and transverse arches
  • Get feet aligned, distribute pressure evenly
  • Custom orthotics (insoles + shoes) for all activities
  • Pick footwear/shoes that fit length & width wise
  • Avoid barefoot activities
  • Cold packs + meds for pain relief

  • Custom Orthotic insoles and footwear to off load the body weight on the bunion
  • Flex Bar or roller for strengthening plantar surface (arches)
  • Toe separator, Toe covers, bunion shield,
  • Bunion splints for support
  • Medical Pedicure, Topical footcare cream to treat and prevent calluses and corns
